Korean Language Programme at InKo Centre

InKo Centre began offering Korean language classes in May 2007. The programme started with a focus on identifying qualified faculty experienced in teaching Korean as a foreign language, and mobilizing first-time learners.

Key partnerships were established with Sangmyung University in 2010 for faculty and student exchange, and with The King Sejong Institute Foundation (KSIF) in 2011 for curriculum support. KSIF is a public institution under the Ministry of Culture, Sports and Tourism, dedicated to managing Korean language and culture education abroad.

Today, InKo Centre offers two major tracks: General Korean (from Beginner to Advanced) and Business Korean (customised for professionals working with Korean firms). The Centre is also a designated venue in South India for TOPIK – the globally recognized Test of Proficiency in Korean.

All courses are developed and quality-checked by KSIF and delivered by native Korean instructors selected by Sangmyung University. Sessions are conducted through secure platforms, with monitoring and evaluation overseen directly by KSIF Korea.
